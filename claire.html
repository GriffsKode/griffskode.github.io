Welcome to Claire's PAW Tracker project!

  • Provides tools to track, visualize, and analyze your pet's breathing rate trends over time.
  • Breathing rate tracking is critical for pets with cardiac issues. Breathing rate should be measured when your pet is asleep. One in ten dogs are estimated to suffer from cardiac related issues.
  • Ordinary Least Squares regression (OLS) is used to determine if there is a correlation between furosemide (diuretic) and breathing rate. This can indicate fluid in their lungs.
  • Breathing rates over 40 per minute can indicate immediate medical attention is required.
  • Input Parameters: Date, Time, Breathing Rate Per Minute, Furosemide Dose, Vetmedin Dose, and Enalapril Dose.
  • Tables and Graphs can be printed to provide to vetenerians to aid with treatment.
  • Breathing Data
  • Python Jupyter Notebook - OLS Regression Test and Plot (8/6/2024 Update)
  • Excel Visualization, Basic Stats, and Pivot Table (8/6/2024 Update)

Background

Claire is a toy maltese and poodle mix that has been one of my best friends for over 12 years. Claire was born with a heart murmur (degenerative mitral valve disease) that developed into heart disease in July 2022, and turned into heart failure in January 2024. Claire was treated in the canine ICU overnight and released into our care the next morning. The treatment for Claire's condition consists of medicine to help her heart function better (vetmedin and enalapril), as well as keep the fluid out of her lungs (furosemide). This same condition is estimated to impact one in ten dogs (NC State Veterinary Hospital). One of the best basic methods for a pet owner to better understand their pet's stability is their resting breathing rate. A dog's resting breathing rate (breaths per minute) is counted when the dog is completely asleep.

This project intends to provide a tool for pet owners to use while tracking their best friend's resting breathing rate. Please consult with vetenerians and professionals to manage your pet's specific condition. Please let me know if you have ideas on how to make the tools more helpful. Thank you!
